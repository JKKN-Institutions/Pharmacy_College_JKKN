## ⚠️ CRITICAL: What You MUST Know Before Starting

### Wikipedia's Core Rules:

1. **Neutral Point of View (NPOV)**: NO promotional language. Write like an encyclopedia, not a brochure.
2. **Verifiability**: EVERY claim needs a reliable published source (citations).
3. **Notability**: The institution must be significant enough for encyclopedic coverage.
4. **No Original Research**: Only include information that's already published elsewhere.
5. **Conflict of Interest**: Disclose if you're affiliated with JKKN (recommended to declare on your user page).

### Common Rejection Reasons:
❌ Promotional tone ("best college", "leading institution")
❌ Insufficient citations
❌ Using the college's own website as the primary source
❌ Reading like advertising copy

## Phase 2: Create Wikipedia Account

**Timeline**: 1 day

### Steps:
1. Go to: https://en.wikipedia.org
2. Click **"Create account"** (top right)
3. Choose username (use your real name or a professional pseudonym, NOT "JKKNCollege" or similar)
4. Provide email address
5. Verify email
6. **Complete 4 edits** to non-controversial articles (this builds trust)
   - Fix typos
   - Add missing references
   - Update outdated information
   - Add wikilinks

### Recommended: Declare Conflict of Interest
On your user page, add:
```
{{COI|JKKN College of Pharmacy}}
I am affiliated with JKKN College of Pharmacy and am creating an article about it.
I will follow all Wikipedia guidelines and welcome editorial review.
```

This transparency actually **helps** your article get approved.

## Phase 3: Draft the Article in Your Sandbox

**Timeline**: 2-3 days

### Steps:

1. **Go to your sandbox**:
   - URL: `https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User:YOUR_USERNAME/sandbox`
   - Or click "Sandbox" link on your user page

2. **Copy the draft from WIKIPEDIA_ARTICLE_DRAFT.md**

3. **Add citations using Wikipedia's citation format**:

**Citation Format Example:**
```
JKKN College of Pharmacy is accredited by NAAC with A Grade.<ref>{{cite web
|url=https://www.naac.gov.in/institutions/accredited-institutions
|title=NAAC Accredited Institutions
|publisher=National Assessment and Accreditation Council
|access-date=2026-02-13
}}</ref>
```

**For news articles:**
```
<ref>{{cite news
|url=https://www.thehindu.com/article-url
|title=Article Title
|newspaper=The Hindu
|date=2024-08-15
|author=Reporter Name
|access-date=2026-02-13
}}</ref>
```

4. **Use Wikipedia's visual editor** for easier formatting:
   - Click "Edit" button in your sandbox
   - Use the toolbar to add references, headings, lists

5. **Add an infobox** (copy from a similar college's Wikipedia page):

## Phase 4: Submit for Review (Articles for Creation)

**Timeline**: 1 day to submit, 2-6 weeks for review

### Steps:

1. **Final check before submission**:
   - ✅ Article has 3+ independent reliable sources
   - ✅ No promotional language
   - ✅ All facts are cited
   - ✅ Neutral tone throughout
   - ✅ Proper formatting (headings, references, categories)
   - ✅ No copyright violations

2. **Add AfC template** at the TOP of your sandbox:
```
{{AFC submission|t||ts=20260213000000|u=YOUR_USERNAME|ns=118}}
```

3. **Or use the AfC Helper Script**:
   - Install: Go to Preferences → Gadgets → Enable "AfC Helper Script"
   - Click "Submit" button that appears in your sandbox

4. **Wait for review**:
   - A Wikipedia editor will review within 1-6 weeks
   - Check your user talk page for feedback
   - Be patient - Wikipedia editors are volunteers

---

## Phase 5: Respond to Reviewer Feedback

**Timeline**: Varies based on feedback

### Common Feedback Types:

#### If APPROVED ✅
- Congratulations! Your article is now live on Wikipedia
- Continue to Phase 6 (Wikidata)

#### If DECLINED with feedback ⚠️
Common requests:
1. **"Needs more sources"**
   - Add 2-3 more independent sources
   - Resubmit

2. **"Tone is promotional"**
   - Remove words like: best, leading, premier, excellent
   - Use neutral language: "established", "offers", "accredited"
   - Resubmit

3. **"Insufficient notability"**
   - Find more news coverage
   - Add government sources
   - Highlight unique achievements
   - If truly insufficient, article may not be approved yet

4. **"Lacks inline citations"**
   - Add `<ref>` tags after each claim
   - Ensure every paragraph has at least one citation

### How to Address Feedback:
1. Read the reviewer's comments carefully
2. Make requested changes
3. Add a note in your sandbox:
   ```
   {{AFC submission|t||ts=20260220000000|u=YOUR_USERNAME|ns=118}}
   I have addressed the reviewer's concerns by:
   - Adding three more independent sources
   - Removing promotional language
   - Adding inline citations throughout
   ```
4. Resubmit

## Phase 6: Create Wikidata Entity

**Timeline**: 1-2 hours

Once your Wikipedia article is APPROVED:

### Steps:

1. Go to: https://www.wikidata.org
2. Create account (can use same credentials as Wikipedia)
3. Click "Create a new item"
4. Fill in using **WIKIDATA_ENTITY_STRUCTURE.md** as guide
5. Link to your Wikipedia article under "Sitelinks"

**Important**: Wikidata is easier to create and doesn't require as strict sourcing, but it's more impactful AFTER the Wikipedia article exists.

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ❌ DON'T:
1. Copy text from the college website (copyright violation)
2. Use promotional language
3. Include unverifiable claims
4. Add excessive detail about courses
5. Include information about individual faculty (unless notable)
6. Submit before gathering proper sources
7. Get defensive if declined - address feedback professionally
8. Abandon the project if declined once - many articles take 2-3 submissions

### ✅ DO:
1. Write in neutral, encyclopedic tone
2. Cite everything with reliable sources
3. Model your article after existing pharmacy college articles
4. Be patient with the review process
5. Respond professionally to feedback
6. Disclose your affiliation
7. Keep content factual and verifiable

## FAQ

**Q: How long does Wikipedia review take?**
A: 1-6 weeks typically, but can be longer during busy periods.

**Q: Can I pay Wikipedia to approve faster?**
A: No. Wikipedia is free and run by volunteers. Paid editing must follow strict disclosure rules.

**Q: What if my article gets rejected?**
A: Address the feedback and resubmit. Many successful articles took 2-3 attempts.

**Q: Do I need to be notable/famous to create a Wikipedia article?**
A: No, YOU don't need to be notable. The INSTITUTION (JKKN) needs to be notable, which it is (NAAC A grade, PCI approved, 35+ years of operation).

**Q: Can I use the college website as a source?**
A: For basic facts (address, phone number), yes. But for notability and main claims, you need independent sources.

**Q: What if someone vandalizes the article?**
A: Wikipedia editors monitor new articles. Vandalism is usually reverted within hours. You can also "watch" the article to get notifications.

**Q: Will this help with Google rankings?**
A: Yes, indirectly. Wikipedia has high domain authority, and a Wikipedia article can appear in search results for your college's name.
