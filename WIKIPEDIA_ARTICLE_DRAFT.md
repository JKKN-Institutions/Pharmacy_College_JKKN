## JKKN College of Pharmacy

**JKKN College of Pharmacy** is a pharmacy college located in Komarapalayam, Tamil Nadu, India. Established in 1987, it is part of the J. K. K. Nattraja Educational Institutions and is approved by the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI) and accredited by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) with an A Grade.

## History

JKKN College of Pharmacy was established in **1987** by the J. K. K. Nattraja Educational Trust in Komarapalayam, Namakkal district, Tamil Nadu.[citation needed] The college received approval from the Pharmacy Council of India (PCI) in **2009** to conduct undergraduate and postgraduate pharmacy programmes.[citation needed]

The institution is part of the larger J. K. K. Nattraja Educational Institutions, which includes engineering, arts and science, and dental colleges.[citation needed]

## Academic Programmes

JKKN College of Pharmacy offers the following programmes:

### Undergraduate Programmes
- **Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm)** – A 4-year undergraduate programme with an intake of 100 students. The programme is affiliated with The Tamil Nadu Dr. M.G.R. Medical University.[citation needed]
- **Pharm.D (Doctor of Pharmacy)** – A 6-year professional doctorate programme with an intake of 30 students, focusing on clinical pharmacy and patient care.[citation needed]

### Postgraduate Programmes
The college offers a 2-year **Master of Pharmacy (M.Pharm)** degree in five specializations:[citation needed]
1. Pharmaceutics (15 seats)
2. Pharmacology (12 seats)
3. Pharmaceutical Chemistry (12 seats)
4. Pharmaceutical Analysis (12 seats)
5. Pharmacy Practice (9 seats)

### Doctoral Programme
- **Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D)** in Pharmaceutical Sciences[citation needed]

All programmes are affiliated with **The Tamil Nadu Dr. M.G.R. Medical University**, Chennai.[citation needed]

## Admissions

Admission to B.Pharm and Pharm.D programmes is conducted through the Tamil Nadu Engineering Admissions (TNEA) counselling process based on marks obtained in the Higher Secondary Certificate (10+2) examination in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics.[citation needed]

For M.Pharm programmes, admission is based on the Graduate Pharmacy Aptitude Test (GPAT) scores and university-specific entrance examinations.[citation needed]

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- **B.Pharm**: 50% marks in 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics (45% for reserved categories)
- **Pharm.D**: 50% marks in 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (45% for reserved categories)
- **M.Pharm**: B.Pharm degree with 55% marks and valid GPAT score (preferred)
